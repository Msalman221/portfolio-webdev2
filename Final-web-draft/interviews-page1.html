Semiotics 101 Interviews How To Examples Reflection
Jorden Smith
An Advertising Strategy/Copywriting specialist at ARC Worldwide, an agency underneath one of the most well-known advertising/marketing conglomerates in the world, the Publicis Groupe.

Synopsis of Interview

Jorden provided some valuable insights into the strategic and creative application of semiotics within advertising. They emphasized the careful selection of symbols and visuals to evoke specific emotional responses, using an example from a P&G campaign where color psychology was employed to convey trust and reliability. Jorden also highlighted how semiotic cues act as 'triggers' in advertising, influencing consumer behavior and purchasing decisions. Our discussion covered the ethical implications of advertising, stressing the necessity for authenticity and transparency in establishing brand loyalty. This interview with Jorden not only enhanced my understanding of semiotics in advertising but also emphasized its critical role in effective marketing strategies.

Key Findings/Quotes From the Interview

  • Throughout the interview, Jorden emphasized the role that semiotics plays in the advertising industry. They explained that "behind every successful campaign, there is a careful selection of symbols and visuals that are designed to evoke specific kinds of emotions and associations to the desired consumer."
  • I asked Jorden about some of the campaigns where they believed that they used semiotics, they mentioned how, "In one fun memorable campaign for our P&G client, we strategically used things like color psychology to evoke feelings of trust and reliability in the in-store ads. This was important to us because that is Tide's message that they want to communicate to consumers. So when we incorporate things like warm tones or familiar imagery, we are able to establish a sense of familiarity and comfort with our desired target audience."
  • One of the key takeaways from my interview with Jorden was Jorden's perspective on how semiotics and consumer psychology work hand in hand. Jorden explained how, "things like semiotic cues can act as what we call in the advertising industry 'triggers', tapping into consumers' subconscious desires and motivations." Their thoughts made me think about the impact of advertising on consumer behavior and purchasing decisions. It also reminded me that just by understanding the nuances of semiotic elements, can help advertisers effectively build connections with their desired target audience for their select client's ask, further fostering things like brand loyalty and engagement for their select client.
  • What Jorden's Interview Tells Us: My interview with Jorden touched on the importance of authenticity and transparency in advertising practices, further emphasizing the need for brands to uphold/stay true to their values and build long-lasting connections with consumers, to make them brand loyalists. All in all, this interview gave us a glimpes into the reaffirmed my beliefs in the profound impact of semiotics in the advertising industry from the perspective of an advertiser.
